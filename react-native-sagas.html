React Native and Sagas

October 27, 2017

When we first started our own Mobile application, we found ourselves in the task of creating almost the same set-up that we were already using for our SPA. React Native was the final choose, along with the most used pattern nowdays, Redux. Not much after we decided to take it a step further implementing the Sagas. There's a couple of implementations of Sagas out there, and even even though for React and Redux.

Development process was, with no preambles, a pain in the ass. For every Component we had to create a thousand files to split the view, logic, and connections. Following and chasing bugs, ended beeing (contrariely to Redux's premise) an horrible task. The store was a good place to start but not really helpful since finally the easiest way to stop a bug from happening was reproducing it step by step.

The idea came to our minds, and it didn't take long until the dream became true. Redux was out of the Project. But what about trackability? Stateless and simple views didnt required a lot of useless data to reproduce the bugs. But we still had the problem of asynchronous operations.

Sagas was first though for driving multiple asyncrhonous sub operations on thouasand of parallel processes, mainly intended for Databases. It was still perfect for us!

We introduced the state of states (no kidding). Every view has, among other state's attributes, a state, which defines how a view should behave at every moment. Let's see it in action.
